## Strategic Notes (for attorney discussion)

- **Broad claim window** — The independent claim uses "any configurable T_total" rather than a fixed duration (e.g. 60 s). This is intentional to maximise defensibility against design-arounds that simply change the window length. If the examiner objects on breadth or enablement grounds, fallback to the 60 s and 120 s preferred embodiments already in dependent claims 4 and 5.

- **Voice profile enrollment is optional** — Enrollment is placed in dependent claim 9, not claim 1, so the core patent is not invalidated by systems that omit a speaker profile. This also aligns with the real product where enrollment improves but does not gate trigger functionality.

- **Sharing / export excluded from claims** — The sharing interface [180] is described in the Detailed Description as an optional embodiment only. Including it in the independent claim would narrow scope unnecessarily and could exclude the core capture-and-index use case conducted entirely on-device.

- **Trigger phrase is user-defined** — Claims deliberately avoid specifying any example trigger word or phrase. Locking to a specific phrase (e.g. "Recall") would allow trivial design-arounds.

- **Method + System + CRM triple** — Claims 1 (method), 2 (system), and 3 (computer-readable medium) provide three independent attack vectors against infringers, covering software, hardware, and distributed embodiments.

## Provisional vs. Complete Application

A **provisional application** establishes the priority date with relatively low cost and no examination. A complete application must follow within 12 months. Given the technology is already in beta, discuss with your attorney whether to file provisional now and complete within the 12-month window, or proceed directly to a complete standard patent application.
